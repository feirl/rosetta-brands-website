The Problem

Most UK brands want to expand. They stop at the complexity.

Post-Brexit EU expansion is genuinely complicated. But the brands that have done it report far less competition and significantly better margins than the UK market. The barrier is operational - and it's entirely removable.

EU VAT registration in up to seven countries. An EORI number. Fiscal representation in France, Spain, Italy and Poland. A GPSR Responsible Person. An EU trademark filed separately from your UK registration. Each one is solvable. Together they look like a full-time job.

5+ registrations before you sell a unit

The first-mover window is closing

EU Amazon category positions are less competed right now than at any point since Brexit. Many UK brands hesitated; many EU brands retreated from the UK. The brands entering Europe in 2025-2026 will own their categories. The ones that wait will pay a premium to catch up.

02 - Pan-European FBA

One pallet. Seven countries. All Prime.

Pan-EU FBA is Amazon's flagship multi-market fulfilment programme. Ship inventory to one EU fulfilment centre and Amazon automatically redistributes it across Germany, France, Italy, Spain, Poland, Netherlands and Belgium - every product instantly Prime-eligible across all seven markets from a single shipment.

  • Up to 53% reduction in fulfilment fees vs. individual country shipping
  • 30% average sales uplift from borderless Prime eligibility
  • 15-25% margin improvement - Rosetta Brands handles logistics routing
  • Post-Brexit: UK inventory cannot serve Pan-EU - EU entry must go direct into EU fulfilment

Common Questions

International expansion - answered

Questions specific to EU expansion through Rosetta Brands. For broader questions about how Vendor-As-A-Service works, visit our FAQ page.

Because Rosetta Brands is already the vendor of record on Amazon, expanding to EU marketplaces means adding those markets to an existing vendor relationship - not building new accounts, new contracts, or new infrastructure from scratch. We manage the Vendor Manager conversations to activate EU access, coordinate VAT registrations and GPSR compliance, set up Pan-European FBA where appropriate, and handle listing localisation. Your brand gets EU presence without the operational overhead.

Pan-European FBA allows a single pallet shipped to one EU Amazon fulfilment centre to make products Prime-eligible across seven countries simultaneously - Germany, France, Italy, Spain, Poland, Netherlands and Belgium. It can reduce fulfilment fees by up to 53% compared to shipping each country individually, and brands typically see a 30% sales uplift from Prime eligibility across all seven markets. Post-Brexit, UK brands cannot use UK FBA warehouses as the Pan-EU distribution hub - inventory must go directly into an EU fulfilment centre. Rosetta Brands handles the logistics routing and inventory strategy.

Amazon.ie launched in March 2025 and is still in its early stages - category positions are far less competed than on Amazon.de or Amazon.fr. Ireland is English-language, which eliminates translation costs and local-language customer service requirements entirely. VAT registration in Ireland provides a legitimate EU VAT number, which serves as the foundation for wider EU expansion. Crucially, Amazon.ie vendor access is not automatically granted to UK vendors - it requires a Vendor Manager conversation. Rosetta Brands' AVS relationships make this conversation possible.

UK brands selling into the EU via Amazon need: VAT registration in each country where Amazon stores Pan-EU inventory; an EU EORI number for importing goods into the EU; GPSR (General Product Safety Regulation) compliance requiring an EU-based Responsible Person; an EU trademark via EUIPO for Brand Registry enrolment (UK trademarks are no longer automatically valid in the EU post-Brexit); and fiscal representation in France, Spain, Italy and Poland for non-EU companies. Rosetta Brands coordinates all of these through trusted advisors and manages the timeline so nothing blocks your launch date.

It depends on the product category, but Ireland (Amazon.ie) is the lowest-friction entry for most UK brands - same language, EU jurisdiction, and category positions that are largely still open. Germany (Amazon.de) is the most commercially significant at $45.9 billion in 2025 - the largest EU Amazon market - and is often the second move. France is strong for food and FMCG. Rosetta Brands conducts a market prioritisation analysis before any expansion - looking at category competition density, pricing headroom and margin potential per market to sequence entry correctly.
