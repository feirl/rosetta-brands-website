The problem with the default

Without a Brand Store, your advertising spend works against you

Most brands send Sponsored Brands traffic to a product detail page. That page is covered in competitor ads. Every pound spent bringing a shopper in is at risk the moment they arrive.

01

Sponsored Brands clicks land on competitor-filled pages

A product detail page is Amazon's page, not yours. Competitor ads run in the sponsored section below your listing, in the "customers also bought" rail, and throughout the page. You paid to bring the shopper in; Amazon's algorithm sends them away. A Brand Store removes all of that, the only products visible are yours.

02

No brand story, no cross-sell, no range discovery

A product page sells one product. A Brand Store sells your brand. Shoppers who arrive via a Sponsored Brands ad and land in your Store can browse your full range, discover complementary products, and increase basket size. For FMCG brands with multiple SKUs, a well-structured Store can meaningfully increase average order value on every campaign click.

03

Stale stores with no seasonal presence are a missed opportunity

Amazon's own data shows that stores updated within the past 90 days achieve up to 35 percent higher attributed sales per visitor compared to stale ones. Brands that refresh their Store for Q4, for a new product launch, or for a key seasonal moment consistently outperform those running the same Store they built two years ago.

Our approach

Built to convert, not just to look good

Most Brand Stores are designed once and forgotten. Ours are built with performance in mind from the outset: structured to convert Sponsored Brands traffic, designed around mobile-first behaviour, and maintained with regular updates that keep Amazon's algorithms and your shoppers engaged.

Depth matters. Amazon's data is unambiguous: stores with 3 or more pages achieve 83 percent higher shopper dwell time and 32 percent higher attributed sales per visitor than single-page stores. Every Brand Store we build starts with the right page architecture.
Strategy & architecture

Page structure designed around how shoppers actually browse

Before a pixel is designed, we define the Store architecture: how many pages, which product categories, how to group your range for maximum cross-sell, and where the brand story lives. For FMCG brands, this typically means a homepage with featured products, two to three range category pages, a best sellers section, and a brand story page. Every navigation decision is made with dwell time and pages-per-visit in mind.

Design & build

On-brand creative, built natively for Amazon's Store Builder

Our creative designer builds the full Store within Amazon's native Store Builder tool, using your brand guidelines to ensure the Store feels like a genuine extension of your brand - not a generic Amazon template. Hero banners, lifestyle imagery sections, product grids, and brand story tiles are all designed for both mobile and desktop simultaneously. With 77 percent of Amazon traffic coming from mobile, mobile optimisation is never an afterthought.

Advertising integration

Wired directly into your Sponsored Brands campaigns

A Brand Store without advertising traffic is a shop with no footfall. We connect your Store directly to your Sponsored Brands campaign strategy, routing ad traffic to the Store homepage or to specific category pages depending on the campaign intent. Product-specific Sponsored Brands ads can point to the relevant category page within your Store, increasing the relevance of the landing experience and improving conversion.

Ongoing updates

Seasonal refreshes and new product integration

A Brand Store is not a one-time build. We refresh your Store for key seasonal moments (Q4 gifting, summer, key trade dates), integrate new product launches, and update hero creative to align with your current campaign calendar. These updates are not just cosmetic: Amazon's data consistently shows that stores refreshed within 90 days outperform stale ones by 11 to 35 percent across repeat visitors and attributed sales per visit.

Analytics & optimisation

Section-level insights that tell you exactly what is working

Amazon now provides section-level analytics within the Brand Store Insights dashboard, showing dwell time, bounce rate, and pages-per-visit broken down by individual Store sections. We use this data to continuously refine the Store: moving high-performing product tiles, adjusting navigation, and identifying which sections drive the most engagement. Your Brand Store improves over time, not just at launch.

Common Questions

Common questions about Amazon Brand Stores

Specific questions about this service. For broader questions about how Rosetta Brands works, visit our FAQ page.

An Amazon Brand Store is a dedicated, multi-page branded storefront within Amazon. It gives your brand a home on the platform, separate from standard product listing pages, where you can present your full product range, share your brand story, and create a shopping experience free from competitor advertising. Brand Stores are accessible via the byline on your product pages, via Sponsored Brands ads, and via a direct Amazon URL for your brand.

No. Creating and maintaining a Brand Store is free. However, the primary driver of traffic to a Brand Store is Sponsored Brands advertising, running ads that link to your Store rather than individual product pages. A well-designed Brand Store without advertising will receive some organic traffic via product page bylines, but the full value is realised when it works in tandem with an active Sponsored Brands campaign strategy.

Sending Sponsored Brands traffic to a Brand Store rather than a product page gives shoppers the opportunity to browse your full range, increasing basket size and the likelihood of a multi-product purchase. It also removes the competitor ads that appear on product detail pages. Amazon's own data shows that stores with 3 or more pages achieve 83 percent higher shopper dwell time and 32 percent higher attributed sales per visitor compared to single-page stores.

Amazon's data shows that stores updated within the past 90 days see up to 35 percent higher attributed sales per visitor compared to stale stores. At a minimum, Brand Stores should be refreshed quarterly, more frequently around key retail moments like Q4, new product launches, or seasonal campaigns. Rosetta Brands manages these updates as part of an ongoing relationship, not as a one-off project.

The Rosetta Brands Brand Store build includes strategic page architecture, full design and build within Amazon's Store Builder, a homepage with brand hero and featured categories, up to five product range pages, a brand story section, mobile optimisation, Sponsored Brands campaign routing, source tag configuration, and Brand Store Insights dashboard setup. The first seasonal refresh within 90 days is also included.
