The Problem

Data is not the same as insight

Amazon Vendor generates a significant amount of data. Most FMCG brands receive it, struggle to interpret it, and end up making strategic decisions with an incomplete picture.

01

Amazon data is complex and hard to act on

Vendor Central surfaces retail analytics, forecasting inputs, and operational data, but interpreting it meaningfully requires significant time and expertise. Most brands receive raw numbers and are left to draw their own conclusions, often without the context to do so accurately.

02

Standard forecasts underestimate your potential

Amazon's projected orders are generated by their own algorithms and regularly fall short for growing brands or products with seasonal variation. Planning to Amazon's numbers means accepting a ceiling that was never calibrated to your business, leading to availability gaps and missed revenue.

03

You don't know what the market is doing

Without external category data and market signals, your Amazon strategy operates in isolation. A flat week could be a brand issue, a category trend, or a seasonal shift, but without broader context, it is impossible to tell. Strategy built on internal data alone is always missing half the picture.

FAQ

Common questions

How is Rosetta's forecasting different from Amazon's standard projections?
Amazon's standard projected orders are based on their own algorithms and often underestimate demand, particularly for growing brands or seasonal products. Rosetta's proprietary forecasting tools combine your historical actuals with advanced modelling to create forecasts that reflect the specific nuances of your business, product range, and growth trajectory. This leads to more accurate planning, better availability, and improved performance over time. We also integrate external data sources that Amazon's projections do not account for.
How often do we receive reporting?
Reporting cadence is built around your business rhythm. Most Rosetta clients receive structured reporting on a weekly or monthly basis, aligned with the commercial decisions that matter most. Data is also available on an ongoing basis through the Client Portal, so you always have access to current performance between scheduled reports, without waiting for a call. The goal is not to flood you with data, but to ensure every conversation is informed by a clear picture of your Amazon performance.
What external data sources does Rosetta incorporate?
Rosetta enhances Amazon Vendor data with external signals including category-level performance trends, wider e-commerce market data, and seasonal intelligence relevant to your product categories. This broader view ensures your strategy is not just reactive to Amazon data, but informed by where the market is heading. It also helps contextualise your own performance, separating what is driven by your brand from what is a category-wide movement.
What Amazon data does Rosetta have access to that we can't see ourselves?
Rosetta's close working relationships with Amazon provide access to data points and insights not typically surfaced through standard Vendor Central access, including category-level benchmarking, retail intelligence shared through Amazon account relationships, and insights from programmes that require an established vendor partnership to access. This gives Rosetta clients a more complete picture and a genuine competitive edge in planning and strategy.
Is the reporting just dashboards, or do we get proper analysis?
Rosetta's approach is deliberately collaborative rather than just self-service. While data is available through the Client Portal at any time, the reporting relationship involves genuine analysis, explaining what the numbers mean, what is driving changes, and what actions to take in response. We work alongside you as a strategic partner, not just a data supplier. The goal is to ensure every decision is informed, understood, and aligned with your commercial goals.
