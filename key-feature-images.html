The Problem

Poor imagery is a silent conversion killer

Most FMCG brands underestimate how much their product photography is costing them — in clicks, in conversions, and in suppressed listings.

Non-compliant images get suppressed without warning

Amazon's image standards are strict — wrong background colour, insufficient product fill, or low resolution (under 1,000px) can trigger automatic listing suppression. Most brands don't know until their sales drop.

Generic product shots leave sales on the table

Shoppers spend 3–5 seconds deciding whether to click. A plain white-background product image tells them nothing about who it's for, how it's used, or why it's different. Your competitors' lifestyle shots are winning that decision.

Unfilled image slots drag down your Listing Media Score

Amazon's Listing Media Score (LMS) rewards brands that maximise all 7 image slots with varied content types. Leaving slots empty — or filling them with near-duplicate angles — costs you ranking points and ad quality scores.

Our Approach

Seven slots, seven jobs to do

Every image in your stack has a specific conversion objective. We plan, brief, and produce each one with that objective front of mind.

1

Compliance audit

Before we shoot anything, we audit your existing images against Amazon's current technical requirements: pure white RGB 255,255,255 background, 85%+ product fill, 2,000px minimum for zoom functionality, correct aspect ratio, and sRGB colour mode. We flag every non-compliant asset and establish exactly what needs to be created or replaced.

2

Image stack strategy

We map each of the 7 image slots to a conversion objective — from the hero main image that stops the scroll, through to the size reference that eliminates the last objection before purchase. Every slot is planned before any photography begins. No filler, no guesswork.

3

Main image excellence

Your main image is your most valuable asset. It appears in search results, Sponsored Product ads, and every placement on Amazon's network. We ensure it's pixel-perfect: pure white background, product fully outside any packaging, correct fill, and sharp at 2,000px for zoom. This image carries your entire click-through rate.

4

Key Feature Images & infographics

Slots 5–6 are where we do our most impactful work: infographic overlays that highlight 3–5 key benefits per SKU, ingredient and material callouts for FMCG products, certification badges, and mobile-first layouts that read clearly at 375px. We focus on outcomes over features — what the product does for the customer, not just what it contains.

5

Lifestyle photography brief

Lifestyle images answer the question "is this for someone like me?" in under a second. We create detailed photography briefs — covering setting, model demographics, props, lighting, and in-use context — tailored to the specific FMCG category. Grocery products photographed in functioning kitchens. Health and beauty shown on real skin. Pet products with real animals in real homes.

Common questions

Everything you need to know about Amazon product imagery before you start.

What's the difference between Key Feature Images and lifestyle photography?
Key Feature Images (KFIs) are infographic-style shots — your product on a clean background with text callouts, ingredient highlights, or benefit overlays. Lifestyle photography shows your product in a real-world setting, in use by a person or in a context that creates emotional resonance. Both types are essential: KFIs build the rational case; lifestyle shots create desire. We plan and produce both as part of a complete 7-slot image stack strategy.
Does Amazon have rules about what can appear in secondary images?
Your main image (Slot 1) has strict rules: pure white background, product outside packaging, no text, logos, or overlays. Secondary images (Slots 2–7) are much more flexible — lifestyle backgrounds, text callouts, infographics, and props are all permitted. However, all images must accurately represent the product, meet the minimum 1,000px requirement (we shoot at 2,000px+), and stay within the 10 MB file size limit. For FMCG specifically, expiry dates and ingredient information must be clearly legible in at least one secondary image.
What is the Listing Media Score and why does it matter?
Amazon's Listing Media Score (LMS) measures the quality and completeness of your product imagery. Listings that fill all 7 image slots with varied content types — multiple angles, lifestyle shots, and infographics — score higher than sparse listings. A higher LMS contributes directly to your listing's search ranking, ad quality scores, and conversion rate. Brands with fewer than 7 images are leaving algorithmic ranking points on the table every day their listing is live.
Can I use the images for other channels — social, email, retail?
Yes — and this is one of the reasons we position our imagery as brand-owned assets. Everything we produce is yours to keep and use across any channel. Lifestyle shots work on Instagram and in email campaigns. Infographic images work in retail brochures and trade presentations. We shoot with Amazon's technical requirements as the floor, not the ceiling, so assets are high-resolution and usable far beyond the listing page.
How does imagery connect to your other Growth Marketing services?
Imagery sits at the core of your entire Amazon content ecosystem. Your A+ Content uses the same lifestyle photography in its modules. Your Brand Store pages are built around your hero imagery and lifestyle creative. Your Sponsored Brand ads pull from your main image and lifestyle shots. When we produce your image stack, we plan it to serve all four channels simultaneously — so you're not commissioning separate photography for each service.
