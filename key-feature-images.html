What's at stake

Poor imagery is a silent conversion killer on every listing

Most consumer brands underestimate how much their product photography is costing them, in clicks, in conversions, and in suppressed listings.

01

Non-compliant images get suppressed without warning

Amazon's image standards are strict, wrong background colour, insufficient product fill, or low resolution under 1,000px can trigger automatic listing suppression. Most brands don't know until their sales drop. A single suppressed main image removes the Buy Box entirely until the issue is resolved.

02

Generic product shots leave sales on the table

Shoppers spend 3–5 seconds deciding whether to click. A plain white-background product image tells them nothing about who it's for, how it's used, or why it's different. Your competitors' lifestyle shots are winning that decision every day, and their conversion rate reflects it.

03

Unfilled image slots drag down your Listing Media Score

Amazon's Listing Media Score rewards brands that fill all 7 image slots with varied content types. Leaving slots empty, or filling them with near-duplicate angles, costs you ranking points and ad quality scores. There is no scenario where fewer images outperform more, assuming quality.

Our approach

Seven slots. Seven jobs to do.

Every image in your stack has a specific conversion objective. We plan each slot before a single image is produced, so there is no filler, no near-duplicate angles, and no guesswork about what each image is supposed to achieve.

Assets you own for life. Everything we produce is brand-owned with full commercial rights. Your images work on Amazon today and across social, email, and retail packaging tomorrow, shot at 2,000px minimum so they're usable at any resolution.
Compliance audit

Finding what is failing before we build anything new

Before we shoot anything, we audit your existing images against Amazon's current technical requirements: pure white RGB 255,255,255 background, 85%+ product fill, 2,000px minimum for zoom functionality, correct aspect ratio, and sRGB colour mode. We flag every non-compliant asset and establish exactly what needs replacing or creating from scratch.

Common Questions

Common questions about Amazon product imagery

Specific questions about this service. For broader questions about how Rosetta Brands works, visit our FAQ page.

Key Feature Images (KFIs) are infographic-style shots, your product on a clean background with text callouts, ingredient highlights, or benefit overlays that communicate specific product features visually. Lifestyle photography shows your product in a real-world setting, in use by a person or in a context that creates emotional resonance. Both are essential: KFIs build the rational case for buying; lifestyle shots create the desire to buy. We produce both as part of a complete 7-slot image stack strategy, planned before any brief is issued.

Your main image (Slot 1) has strict rules: pure white background (RGB 255,255,255), product outside packaging, no text or logos, and 85%+ product fill. Secondary images (Slots 2–7) are much more flexible, lifestyle backgrounds, text callouts, infographics, and props are all permitted. However, all images must accurately represent the product, meet the minimum 1,000px requirement (we shoot at 2,000px+), and stay within the 10 MB file size limit. For consumer specifically, expiry dates and ingredient information must be clearly legible in at least one secondary image.

Amazon's Listing Media Score (LMS) measures the quality and completeness of your product imagery. Listings that fill all 7 image slots with varied content types, multiple angles, lifestyle shots, and infographics, score higher than sparse listings. A higher LMS contributes directly to your listing's search ranking, ad quality scores, and conversion rate. Brands with fewer than 7 images are leaving algorithmic ranking points on the table every day their listing is live. There is no scenario where fewer images outperform more, assuming the additional images are high quality.
