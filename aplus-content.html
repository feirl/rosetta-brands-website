What's at stake

A standard listing is a missed opportunity at every price point

For FMCG brands selling on Amazon, the product detail page is the moment of decision. Most brands hand that moment to a wall of text and hope for the best.

01

Unanswered objections send shoppers to a competitor

On Amazon, the shopper who does not buy does not leave empty-handed, they add a competitor's product to cart instead. For FMCG brands, conversion rates of 30 to 35 percent are achievable, but only when the listing gives shoppers enough information to act. Every unanswered question about ingredients, usage, or quality is a sale lost to the brand below yours on the page.

02

Standard listings break on mobile, where most shoppers are

Over 70 percent of Amazon traffic is mobile. A bullet-point product description that reads clearly on desktop becomes a wall of text on a phone screen. Text baked into images becomes unreadable when scaled down. Most brands have never checked what their listing actually looks like on the device their customers are using. The answer is rarely encouraging.

03

Generic listings do not build brand equity, they erode it

A product detail page with a white background photo and a five-bullet description signals nothing about why your brand is different. For premium FMCG brands, a generic listing actively undermines the perception you have built everywhere else. The listing is not just where sales are won or lost, it is where brand equity is tested at the precise moment a shopper is deciding whether to trust you.

Our approach

Strategy first. Design second.

The most common mistake with A+ Content is treating it as a design project. It is not. It is a conversion problem. We begin by identifying the specific objections that are stopping your shoppers from buying, then build content that addresses those objections directly, in the format, order, and visual language that works for your category.

Premium A+ where eligible. Objection analysis

Finding what is stopping shoppers from buying

Before a single module is designed, we review the customer Q&A, one and two star reviews, and competitor listing weaknesses for your category. This reveals the specific questions shoppers are asking that your current listing is not answering. Ingredients, portion size, taste, quality provenance, usage occasion, the objections vary by category but the principle is the same: content that does not address the real questions does not convert.

Brand Story module

The first thing shoppers see, above your A+ Content

The Brand Story module sits directly above your A+ Content on every product page and is the only section that appears on all your listings simultaneously. It introduces your brand, who you are, why you exist, what makes you different, and includes a direct link to your Brand Store. For FMCG brands building long-term loyalty, the Brand Story module is the most efficient brand-building real estate on Amazon: one build, every product page, always on.

Lifestyle & feature modules

Imagery and copy that does the work a bullet list cannot

Each A+ module is designed to address a specific objection or reinforce a specific brand value. A lifestyle image showing the product in its natural usage context answers the "what is this actually like?" question that no amount of product specification can. A feature highlight block with iconography addresses the "what makes this worth the price?" objection. Every module earns its place by doing a specific conversion job.

Lifestyle imagery Feature highlight tiles Ingredient callouts Usage occasion shots Quality credentials
Comparison chart

The cross-sell module that grows basket size

The comparison chart module lets you place up to six of your own products side by side across up to twelve attributes. For FMCG brands with multiple SKUs, different flavours, sizes, formats, or use cases, a well-built comparison chart does two things: it helps shoppers find the right product for them, and it introduces them to products they did not know you made. In Premium A+, the chart is shoppable, with Add to Cart buttons for each product in the grid.

Common Questions

Common questions about Amazon A+ Content

Specific questions about this service. For broader questions about how Rosetta Brands works, visit our FAQ page.

Amazon A+ Content is a feature available to brand-registered vendors and sellers that allows them to replace the standard product description on a listing with rich multimedia content, lifestyle imagery, brand storytelling, comparison charts, video, and interactive modules. It appears in the "From the manufacturer" section of a product detail page and is designed to reduce purchase hesitation, answer customer questions, and increase conversion rate. Amazon's own data shows Basic A+ Content can increase sales by up to 8 percent and Premium A+ by up to 20 percent.

Basic A+ Content allows up to 5 modules per product page from a set of standard image and text tiles. Premium A+ Content adds 19 further module types including full-width video, interactive image hotspots, enhanced comparison charts with Add to Cart buttons, and larger image formats up to 1,464 pixels wide. Premium A+ is available to eligible vendors and brand-registered sellers who have published a Brand Story and had at least 5 A+ Content projects approved in the past year. Amazon's data shows Premium A+ can deliver up to 20 percent sales uplift versus 8 percent for Basic.

A+ Content does not directly affect keyword ranking on Amazon. However, it has a significant indirect impact: better content reduces purchase hesitation, which improves conversion rate, which in turn improves sales velocity, a factor Amazon's algorithm does weight. Brands with strong A+ Content typically see better conversion rates, fewer returns, and higher review scores, all of which contribute to long-term ranking improvement.

Effective A+ Content for FMCG brands addresses the specific objections that stop shoppers from purchasing, questions about ingredients, usage, portion size, taste, quality credentials, or how the product compares to alternatives. It should include lifestyle imagery showing the product in context, a comparison chart featuring the full product range, a Brand Story element that builds trust, and copy that is mobile-optimised and scannable. Generic brand content without a clear objection-handling strategy produces minimal uplift.

A+ Content, the Brand Store, and advertising work as an integrated content ecosystem on Amazon. Sponsored Brands ads drive shoppers to the Brand Store, where they browse the full range. When they click through to individual product pages, A+ Content is what converts them. The Brand Story module within A+ Content also links back to the Store, creating a loop that keeps shoppers within your brand world throughout their journey.
